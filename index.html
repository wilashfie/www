Research

My research interests cover solar flares, magnetic reconnection, and energy transport in the solar atmosphere.

Previous work has looked into the characteristic downflows seen in the lower atmosphere during the impulsive phase of flares — known as chromospheric condensation — to infer properties of flare energy release. You can read more about these projects here.

Currently, I am helping to develop a model that produces the heating required to sustain coronal EUV emission during the gradual phase of flares. The mechanism we propose is the slow dissipation of turbulent Alfv√©n waves initiated during the retractin of newly-reconnected flare loops through a current sheet.
